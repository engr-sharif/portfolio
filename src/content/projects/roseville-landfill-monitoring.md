## Setting

A closed municipal solid waste (MSW) landfill in California's Central Valley
(Placer County / Sacramento metro) remains under long-term **post-closure
monitoring** governed by **CCR Title 27** and a Regional Water Quality Control
Board Waste Discharge Requirements / Monitoring & Reporting Program order. The
site requires recurring **semiannual** demonstration that groundwater, surface
water, and landfill gas remain within regulatory limits across a multi-well
compliance network and adjacent surface-water and perimeter gas-monitoring
points.

## My role

I serve as **author and data-evaluation lead** for the semiannual water-quality
monitoring report — translating field events and laboratory results into a
regulator-ready compliance narrative. I coordinate inputs from field sampling,
laboratory chemistry, and figures/tables, and prepare the draft for project
manager and client review prior to PE certification and agency submittal.

## Methods & approach

Work spans a defined seasonal field event (groundwater, surface water, and
landfill-gas monitoring with instruments such as the **Landtec GEM 5000** and a
**MiniRAE 3000 PID**), followed by structured **data evaluation** — trend review
against multi-year historical context, background-versus-compliance comparison,
and QA/QC validation. I adapt a controlled cyclic report template and apply a
disciplined "diff every value against its source" review with color-coded
change tracking to catch stale carryover text — a common quality risk in
recurring regulatory reporting.

## Outcome

A defensible, internally consistent semiannual compliance report suitable for
client review and agency submittal, with corrective-action effectiveness
assessed against historical trends.

## Skills demonstrated

Regulatory compliance reporting; multi-media environmental monitoring
(groundwater / surface water / landfill gas); field instrumentation; data QA/QC
and trend analysis; technical writing for regulatory audiences; and custom
Python tooling to automate paragraph-level diffing and QC of cyclic reports.
